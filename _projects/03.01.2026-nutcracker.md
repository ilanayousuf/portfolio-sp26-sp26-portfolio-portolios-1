**Problem Statement and Objective:**

Find the dimensions and design of a nutcracker based on typical macadamia nut size, maximum grip strength, force required, etc. 

**Constraints and Input Parameters:**

Maximum grip strength: 82 lbs
Macadamia nut diameter: 1 inch
Average load required to crack a macadamia nut: 490 lbf
Average width of human hand: 7 inches

**Approach to the problem:**

Plan:
1) Using values of macadamia nut size and grip strength, create an overall FBD with simple geometry of a potential nutcracker design
2) Create an exploded FBD of one member of the nutcracker
3) Solve for the required size of the lever using the sum of moments about point A (as shown in drawn fbd)
4) Using the average width of a hand, calculate the entire length of the nutcracker

see FBDs and calculations in /assets/images/nutcracker-approach.jpg

**Design Usability:**
This design has a mechanical advantage of 490/82 = 5.98. The user's input force is multiplied by ~6 to crack the nut, due to the ratio between the size of the entire nutcracker (d) to the length between the fulcrum and applied load (x). Additionally, the the calculated final length of my nutcracker is d = 8.407 inches, which falls within the typical range of a nutcracker: 6-10 inches. Finally, since the length between the fulcrum and load is equal to the size of a human hand, the nutcracker should be comfortable to use.
